## 7. Observations

### SERP feature density

- **Local pack (GBP 3-pack):** Present on all Chicago-modifier queries. Dominant above-fold element. Essence must invest in GBP reviews/posts specifically tagged to acne services.
- **Yelp "Top 10" listicles:** Rank #1–#3 for every competitive Chicago acne variant. Essence should be optimized on Yelp (category "Skin Care," tagged keywords: acne, extractions, teen acne, back facial).
- **Fresha listing pages:** Present on mid-tail queries with price bands — aggregator pages ranking via schema. Implementing Service + Offer schema with price on Essence's acne pages mirrors this pattern.
- **Featured snippets:** Most commonly pulled from informational blog posts (Healthline, AAD, Medical News Today, Cleveland Clinic). Opportunity for Essence: FAQ-schema rich Q&A blocks on service pages + a true pillar article.
- **People Also Ask:** Dense on every informational query — 4–8 PAA expansions typical. Heavy FAQ coverage on the sub-pillar page is critical.
- **Video pack:** Sparse. Not a priority channel for this sub-pillar.
- **Shopping/Products:** Not triggered for service queries.
- **AI Overviews:** Triggered on "what is / how often / does it work" queries. AI Overviews cite Healthline, AAD, Hydrafacial.com, Cleveland Clinic. To be cited, Essence needs: clear definitional H2s, short paragraph answers (≤45 words), citable stats, Face Reality / Hydrafacial brand mentions, and published authorship.

### Dominant content format

1. **Service pages with embedded FAQ** — Advanced Dermatology, SpaDerma, Skin Bar. Most rankers.
2. **Condition-focused long-form** — Chicago Skin Clinic's /acne/ page; ~2,000 words, combines info + treatment menu + CTA.
3. **Blog/editorial explainers** — Pure Medical Spa "How to Treat Hormonal Acne" pattern.
4. **Directory listicles** — Yelp/Fresha/Groupon dominate pure commercial queries.
5. **Comparison posts** — "Chemical peel vs facial" content format owned by out-of-market blogs; Chicago competitive gap.

### Content length range of top 10 results

- Service pages: 600–1,400 words (median ~900).
- Condition / pillar pages: 1,500–2,500 words.
- Blog explainers: 1,200–2,000 words.
- Essence's existing Deep Pore Cleansing and Express Teen pages are on the short end — opportunity to enrich with FAQ + comparison sections without disrupting conversion CTA flow.

- **Teen acne facial Chicago** → #2 organic (`/services/facial-treatments/express-teen/`). Defend + expand.

- **Acne facial Jefferson Park / acne treatment Jefferson Park** → Essence does NOT appear on page 1 despite being the best-matched local business. Biggest hyper-local opportunity in this sub-pillar.
- **Hormonal acne, cystic acne, back acne (bacne)** → No dedicated Essence pages. Pure-green whitespace for new long-tail landing pages under the sub-pillar.

### Strategic gaps to close (ranked)

1. **Hub/sub-pillar page:** `/services/facial-treatments/acne-and-clarity/` — catches head term, links down to Acne & Oil Control, Deep Pore Cleansing, Express Teen, Back Facial, Chemical Peels.
2. **Comparison content:** "Acne facial vs chemical peel vs HydraFacial — which is right for you?" — high PAA density, no Chicago-market ranker.
3. **Condition-first pages:** Hormonal acne, cystic acne, bacne, blackheads — each gets a short condition explainer linking to the treatment menu.
4. **Hyper-local signal:** Jefferson Park / Milwaukee Ave / 60630 mentions on every acne page; neighborhood landing variants for Edgebrook, Norwood Park, Portage Park, Forest Glen.
5. **FAQ schema everywhere:** 8–12 PAA-aligned Qs per service page.

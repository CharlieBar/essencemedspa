## Understanding Why Hair Thins

Before exploring solutions, it helps to understand the mechanism. Hair loss is rarely one thing — it's typically a combination of factors that tip the balance between hair growth and shedding.

### The Hair Growth Cycle

Each hair follicle cycles through three phases:
- **Anagen (growth phase):** Lasting 2–7 years, this is when the hair shaft actively grows
- **Catagen (transition phase):** A brief 2–3 week phase where growth stops and the follicle shrinks
- **Telogen (resting/shedding phase):** The hair is released and the follicle rests before restarting

In healthy hair, roughly 85–90% of follicles are in anagen at any time. Hair thinning occurs when follicles spend progressively more time in telogen and less in anagen — producing shorter, finer hairs and eventually miniaturizing to the point of dormancy.

### Common Triggers

- **Androgenetic alopecia** (pattern hair loss): The most common type in both men and women; DHT sensitivity causes follicle miniaturization over time
- **Telogen effluvium:** Stress, illness, nutritional deficiencies, or hormonal shifts push follicles prematurely into the resting phase
- **Scalp inflammation:** Poor circulation, product buildup, and inflammatory conditions create an environment hostile to follicle health
- **Nutritional gaps:** Iron, zinc, biotin, and adequate protein are all essential for the energy-intensive anagen phase

The good news: for most types of hair thinning — particularly in the earlier stages — the follicles themselves are not dead. They're dormant or compromised. That's precisely the opening that regenerative treatments exploit.

## PRP for Hair Restoration: The Science

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) is derived from your own blood. A small sample is drawn, spun in a centrifuge to concentrate the platelet layer, and then injected directly into the scalp at the follicle level.

Why does this matter? Platelets are loaded with growth factors that your body uses to repair and regenerate tissue:

- **PDGF** stimulates cell proliferation and tissue repair
- **VEGF** promotes new blood vessel formation — improving scalp circulation and nutrient delivery to follicles
- **IGF-1** supports follicle survival and extends the anagen growth phase
- **TGF-beta** plays a role in regulating the hair cycle

Research published in peer-reviewed dermatology journals has demonstrated that PRP injections significantly increase hair count, hair thickness, and the proportion of follicles in the growth phase in patients with androgenetic alopecia. It's not speculation — it's one of the better-documented non-surgical interventions available.

### The PRP Treatment Session

A typical PRP hair restoration session runs 45–60 minutes:

1. Blood is drawn from the arm — a small sample, quick and routine
2. The centrifuge concentrates the platelet-rich layer over approximately 10 minutes
3. The scalp is cleaned and a topical numbing agent applied if desired
4. PRP is injected in a grid pattern across the areas of thinning using very fine needles
5. A brief scalp massage may follow to encourage distribution

Most clients describe the sensation as mildly uncomfortable — a series of small pinches — rather than painful. Those with lower pain tolerance can request topical numbing.

### The Treatment Protocol

Results from a single PRP session are limited. A standard protocol involves:

- **Initial phase:** 3–4 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart
- **Maintenance phase:** 1–2 sessions annually to sustain and build on results

The biology is cumulative. Each session adds to the growth factor concentration in the scalp environment, progressively improving follicle function. Most clients begin noticing reduced shedding after sessions 2–3, with visible density improvements appearing around months 4–6 of the initial protocol.

## Realistic Expectations: What PRP Can and Cannot Do

### What PRP Does Well

- Slowing or halting progressive hair thinning in its early-to-moderate stages
- Increasing density in areas of diffuse thinning
- Improving hair shaft caliber (individual strands become thicker and stronger)
- Extending the growth phase of existing follicles
- Creating a better scalp environment for sustained follicle health

### Honest Limitations

PRP cannot create new follicles where none exist. Areas of complete baldness — where follicles have fully miniaturized and the scalp has fibrosed — will not respond to PRP. This is why early intervention matters so much: the sooner you address thinning, the more follicular capital you have to work with.

PRP also requires consistency. Missing sessions or abandoning maintenance treatments will result in gradual return to pre-treatment patterns over 12–18 months.

## Practical Tips to Maximize Your Results

**Before treatment:**
- Arrive well-hydrated — blood hydration directly affects PRP yield and concentration
- Avoid blood-thinning supplements (fish oil, vitamin E, NSAIDs) for 5–7 days prior
- Wash your hair the morning of your appointment with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
- Come prepared to discuss your full medical history, medications, and any supplements you take

**After treatment:**
- Avoid washing your hair for 24 hours post-treatment
- No vigorous exercise, saunas, or excessive sweating for 24–48 hours
- Avoid applying styling products or dry shampoo directly to the scalp for 48 hours
- Sleep on a clean pillowcase

**Between sessions:**
- Commit to a scalp-friendly hair care routine: gentle shampoos, no harsh chemical treatments
- Consider adding [PRP facial](/services/prp-facial) to your aesthetic routine — the same growth factors that regenerate your scalp also rejuvenate facial skin
- Optimize protein intake: hair is made of keratin, a protein, and inadequate dietary protein impairs the anagen phase
- Address any underlying iron or thyroid issues with your primary care physician

## Your Skin Changes with the Seasons — Your Care Should Too

Chicago delivers four dramatically different seasons, and each one creates a distinct set of challenges for your skin. The bone-dry winters strip moisture and compromise the skin barrier. Spring brings fluctuating temperatures and allergen exposure that trigger sensitivity. Summer means sustained UV exposure, humidity-driven congestion, and oil production that can undo even a well-established routine. Fall offers a brief, ideal window for corrective treatments before winter arrives and closes that opportunity.

Seasonal skincare for Chicago residents is not optional maintenance — it is a practical response to an extreme climate. What works in August will actively damage skin in February. What you need in March is different from what October demands. This guide walks through each season with specific guidance on at-home adjustments and professional treatments that match what your skin faces throughout the year.

## Winter (December–February): Protect and Restore

Chicago winters are among the most skin-hostile conditions in any major American city. Outdoor temperatures regularly fall below freezing, indoor heating systems strip humidity from the air, and wind chill accelerates transepidermal water loss — the process by which moisture escapes through your skin barrier. The result is the classic Chicago winter complexion: tight, flaky, dull, and reactive.

### What Is Actually Happening to Your Skin

Cold air holds less moisture than warm air, so outdoor exposure immediately starts pulling hydration from the surface layers of the skin. When you move indoors, forced-air heating drops humidity levels further — often to 20% or lower, well below the 40–60% range skin functions best in. The skin barrier, a lipid-rich protective layer, cracks under this sustained assault. Once the barrier is compromised, irritants penetrate more easily and moisture escapes faster, creating a cycle that most over-the-counter products cannot break on their own.

### At-Home Adjustments for Winter

Switch your cleanser to a cream or oil-based formula — foaming cleansers disrupt the barrier further. Apply your moisturizer immediately after washing while skin is still slightly damp, locking the remaining water into the surface layers. Choose products with ceramides, hyaluronic acid, and squalane rather than the lightweight gels that work well in summer. A bedroom humidifier running overnight makes a measurable difference in how skin feels by morning.

Do not stop wearing SPF. Cloudy winter skies still transmit UV radiation — particularly UVA, which penetrates glass and drives collagen breakdown regardless of temperature. A broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher applied every morning is non-negotiable, year-round.

### Professional Winter Treatments

A professional [hydrating facial](/services/hydrating-facial) mid-winter delivers infusions of hyaluronic acid, peptides, and barrier-support ingredients at concentrations that at-home products cannot match. The treatment also allows a licensed aesthetician to assess your barrier integrity and adjust your home protocol based on what they find — not what a label recommends for a generic skin type.

Winter is also the right time to address pigmentation and dullness that has accumulated over the prior year. A [brightening facial](/services/brightening-facial) uses targeted actives to lift surface discoloration while supporting circulation and luminosity — the combination that gives Chicago clients that rested, glowing look in what is objectively the most skin-stressful season.

## Spring (March–May): Reset and Repair

By March, most Chicago skin has been through months of barrier stress. Spring is the reset window — temperatures are rising, UV exposure is not yet at peak summer levels, and the skin's natural renewal cycle accelerates with increasing daylight. This is the season to remove winter's accumulated damage and prepare the canvas for the months ahead.

### What Is Actually Happening to Your Skin

Cell turnover, which naturally slows in cold weather, picks back up as temperatures warm. Dead skin cells that have been sitting on the surface all winter are ready to shed — but often need help. Pores that were relatively quiescent in winter start reacting to pollen and increased humidity, and the shift between cold mornings and warm afternoons stresses barrier function in a different way than full winter does.

### At-Home Adjustments for Spring

Reintroduce exfoliation gradually — once or twice a week with a gentle acid (lactic or mandelic acid suit most skin types) rather than jumping straight back to daily retinol or aggressive scrubs. Begin increasing your SPF if you scaled back during winter months. Transition toward a lighter moisturizer as outdoor humidity climbs, but do not switch abruptly — let your skin guide the timing.

Vitamin C serum is particularly valuable in spring: it provides antioxidant protection against increasing UV exposure, supports collagen synthesis after a stressful winter, and actively works on the pigmentation that cold months can intensify.

### Professional Spring Treatments

Spring is the right time for a [custom peel](/services/custom-peel) — a professionally formulated exfoliation treatment that removes winter's dead cell accumulation and jump-starts the renewal process. Unlike at-home exfoliants, a clinical peel is matched to your current skin condition: the specific acids, the concentration, and the contact time are all calibrated to deliver results without provoking the sensitivity that winter-stressed skin is prone to.

Following a peel with a [brightening facial](/services/brightening-facial) a few weeks later creates a two-stage spring protocol that is highly effective: the peel clears the surface while the brightening treatment works on the underlying pigmentation and supports the even, luminous tone that makes summer skin look its best.

## Summer (June–August): Defend and Control

Chicago summers bring their own complications. High humidity increases oil production and sweat-driven pore congestion. UV index peaks from late June through August, and the combination of sunscreen, sweat, and environmental pollutants creates a barrier on the skin that, if not thoroughly removed each evening, leads to breakouts and dullness.

### What Is Actually Happening to Your Skin

Heat stimulates sebaceous glands to produce more oil, and humidity slows the natural evaporation that normally keeps oil levels in check. The result is congestion — not necessarily acne, but clogged pores, enlarged pore appearance, and a texture that looks rough even on otherwise clear skin. UV exposure meanwhile triggers melanin production, and the city's pollution load adds oxidative stress that accelerates visible aging.

### At-Home Adjustments for Summer

Switch to a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer and an SPF 50 broad-spectrum sunscreen applied every morning, with reapplication at midday if you are spending time outdoors. Double cleanse every evening: an oil-based cleanser first to dissolve sunscreen and sebum, then a water-based cleanser to remove everything else. Avoid heavy occlusive products at night in summer — your skin does not need the barrier support it needs in winter.

Clay masks once a week help manage oil production without stripping. Keep your Vitamin C in the routine — summer UV makes antioxidant protection more important, not less.

### Professional Summer Treatments

The [oxygen facial](/services/oxygen-facial) is exceptionally well-suited to summer. It delivers high-concentration oxygen infused with hyaluronic acid and skin-specific actives under pressure, instantly calming inflammation, clearing surface congestion, and providing the hydration that air-conditioned environments remove. There is zero downtime, no sensitivity risk in sun-exposed skin, and the result is immediate luminosity — making it the ideal pre-event treatment throughout the summer season.

A [hydrating facial](/services/hydrating-facial) in summer might seem counterintuitive if you are oily, but dehydration and oiliness often coexist. Air conditioning dehydrates skin while heat stimulates oil. A hydrating facial targeted to combination summer skin addresses both issues simultaneously, restoring water balance without adding heaviness.

## Fall (September–November): Correct and Strengthen

Fall is the professional's favorite season for skincare intervention, and for good reason. UV exposure drops sharply after Labor Day. Temperatures are manageable and consistent. The skin has had months of barrier support and is in its most resilient condition. And winter — with its inevitable barrier assault — is approaching. This is the window to do corrective work.

### What Is Actually Happening to Your Skin

Pigmentation accumulated over summer is fully visible in fall lighting. The skin's repair mechanisms are active and responsive. Lower UV exposure means that treatments requiring sun avoidance during recovery — chemical peels, resurfacing, any treatment that temporarily compromises the skin surface — carry less risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

### At-Home Adjustments for Fall

Begin transitioning back toward richer moisturizers as outdoor temperatures drop. If you have not been using a retinol consistently, fall is the ideal time to reintroduce it or increase your concentration — lower UV means lower retinol sensitivity risk. Add a facial oil to your evening routine as temperatures start to dip, applying it over your moisturizer to seal in hydration.

Begin rebuilding your barrier proactively before winter arrives. Ceramide-rich products used consistently through October give the barrier the raw materials it needs to withstand December and January.

### Professional Fall Treatments

Fall is prime time for a [custom peel](/services/custom-peel) series. Deeper peel formulations that address actinic pigmentation, uneven texture, and fine lines are most safely performed when sun exposure is minimal and healing conditions are optimal. A series of three peels spaced three to four weeks apart — beginning in September and completing in November — produces significant improvements in skin clarity, tone evenness, and texture that sustain through winter.

The [oxygen facial](/services/oxygen-facial) also performs well in fall as a standalone brightening treatment between more intensive procedures, maintaining luminosity during the corrective phase without adding downtime.

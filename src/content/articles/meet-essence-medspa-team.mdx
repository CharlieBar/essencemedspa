## The People Behind Every Result

When you sit down in the treatment chair at Essence Medspa & Wellness Center, you are not just receiving a service — you are placing your trust in a team that takes that responsibility seriously. Located in Chicago's Edgebrook neighborhood, Essence has built its reputation treatment by treatment, client by client, because of the people delivering the care. Understanding who they are and how they work gives you confidence before your first appointment and clarity about why results here look different.

This is not a team that defaulted into aesthetics. Every licensed specialist on our staff made a deliberate choice to pursue advanced training in medical-grade skincare, wellness, and body contouring. That intentionality shows in the way they consult, the way they listen, and the precision they bring to every procedure.

## A Philosophy Built Around Long-Term Skin Health

The Essence team operates on a principle that separates genuine medspa professionals from trend-chasers: they are here to serve your skin's long-term health, not just the result you want by next Saturday.

That means consultations at Essence run longer than most. Before recommending [Botox](/services/botox) or any injectable treatment, the team takes time to understand your facial anatomy, your medical history, your lifestyle, and your goals. Before scheduling a [custom facial](/services/custom-facial), an aesthetician will analyze your skin under magnification — assessing hydration levels, pore congestion, pigmentation patterns, and sensitivity — then build a protocol around what your skin actually needs rather than a generic menu option.

This approach requires more preparation and more conversation. The team embraces that. They have seen enough clients who arrived with skin damage from treatments that were too aggressive, products that were wrong for their barrier type, or procedures that were performed without proper assessment. Restoring trust, as well as skin, takes time. Essence would rather do it right the first visit.

### Credentials That Go Beyond the Minimum

Illinois requires licensed aestheticians to complete a minimum number of training hours before practicing. The Essence team treats that as a floor, not a ceiling.

Every specialist on staff pursues continuing education through advanced certification programs, manufacturer-led training for the devices and injectables used in-house, and industry conferences that keep them current with evolving protocols. When a new technology — a new RF microneedling handpiece, a refined HydraFacial delivery method, a newly approved injectable — enters the market, the team trains on it before it enters the service menu. Clients are never test subjects for techniques the team has not already mastered.

This commitment extends to safety protocols. Medical-grade treatments require medical-grade oversight. The structure at Essence ensures that every injectable service, every device-based treatment, and every advanced skin procedure is performed within a framework that prioritizes client safety above upselling or throughput.

## What a Consultation Actually Looks Like

Many clients arrive at Essence having already done their research — they know they want a [HydroFacial](/services/hydrofacial), or they have seen dermal filler results on social media and want something similar. The team welcomes that starting point. But they also know that what a client wants and what a client's skin needs are not always the same thing, and the consultation is where those two things are reconciled.

A typical skin consultation at Essence begins with a structured intake covering your current home routine, any prescription topicals or medications, recent treatments elsewhere, known sensitivities, and your primary concerns. The aesthetician will then perform a hands-on skin analysis — looking, feeling, and sometimes using magnification tools — before making any recommendations.

The result is a treatment plan, not just a single appointment. Clients who see the most dramatic improvements are those who work with the team over multiple visits, adjusting protocols as the skin responds and progresses. The team communicates this clearly from the start: some goals take a series of treatments, and pretending otherwise would be doing you a disservice.

### Listening as a Clinical Skill

One of the most underrated aspects of an excellent aesthetician is the ability to listen — not just to what a client says, but to what they mean. A client who says "I just want to look less tired" is communicating something different from one who says "I want to look ten years younger." A client who is nervous about needles needs a different approach than one who is procedure-savvy and focused on efficiency.

The Essence team trains for this. They pay attention to language, body language, and the emotional subtext of what clients share about their skin. Skin is personal. It is tied to self-image, confidence, and sometimes years of frustration with conditions that have not responded to previous treatments. The team approaches those conversations with care, not a script.

## Training Standards That Protect You

Choosing a medspa is a medical decision, even when the treatments feel like pampering. The qualifications of the person performing your treatment directly affect your safety and your results.

At Essence, every service is matched to a team member with the appropriate licensure and training for that specific procedure. Injectables are administered by licensed professionals trained in facial anatomy and injection technique. Advanced device treatments — RF microneedling, Jet Plasma, HydraFacial — are performed by specialists who have completed device-specific certification, not just general aesthetician training.

This structure is not bureaucratic — it is protective. It means you will never receive a treatment from someone who is learning on the job at your expense. It means that when something unexpected happens during a session — a sensitivity reaction, an unexpected skin response — the team knows exactly how to handle it.

### Ongoing Education as a Team Commitment

The aesthetics industry evolves quickly. New research on skin biology, new peptide formulations, new delivery technologies, new injection techniques — the science does not stand still, and neither does the Essence team.

Continuing education is not left to individual initiative. It is structured into how the practice operates. When a team member completes advanced training in a new modality, that knowledge is shared across the team. When protocols change based on new clinical evidence, the whole team updates their approach. This collective learning model means that every client — whether they have been coming to Essence for five years or five days — benefits from the full depth of the team's combined expertise.

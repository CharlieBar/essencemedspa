## What Botox Actually Does (And What It Doesn't)

If you're considering Botox for the first time, you probably have questions — and maybe some misconceptions. Let's clear those up.

Botox Cosmetic is an FDA-approved injectable that temporarily relaxes specific facial muscles. When those muscles relax, the wrinkles they create — forehead lines, crow's feet, frown lines — soften and smooth out. It doesn't add volume (that's fillers), it doesn't change your bone structure, and it doesn't freeze your face when done properly.

The key phrase there is "when done properly." Botox results depend entirely on the skill of your injector — how well they understand facial anatomy, how precisely they dose, and how they customize placement for your unique face.

## How It Feels: Honest Expectations

The needle used for Botox is a 32-gauge — thinner than a human hair. Most clients describe the sensation as a brief pinch or mosquito bite. The entire injection process takes 10-15 minutes.

Here's what happens in real time at our Chicago studio: your injector maps your facial muscles during the consultation, marking where each injection will go. The treatment areas are cleaned. Then a series of small injections are placed — typically 3-5 injection points per area. Most people don't need numbing cream, but we offer it if you're nervous.

After the injections, you'll have tiny bumps at each site that flatten within 15-30 minutes. You can return to work or errands immediately.

## When You'll See Results

Botox doesn't work instantly. Here's the realistic timeline:

Day 1-2: Nothing visible yet. The toxin is binding to nerve receptors. Day 3-5: You'll start noticing the muscles feel slightly different when you try to make expressions. Day 7-10: Visible smoothing becomes apparent. Day 14: Full results. This is when your injector evaluates and makes any adjustments if needed.

Results last 3-4 months for most clients. Over time, many people find they can extend the interval as their muscles become trained to relax.

## Aftercare: What to Do (and Not Do)

The first 24 hours matter most. Avoid rubbing the treated areas, lying face-down, or doing intense exercise. These precautions prevent the Botox from migrating away from the intended muscles.

After 24 hours, you can resume all normal activities. There's no special skincare routine required, though we always recommend daily SPF to protect your investment.

## Is Botox Right for You?

Botox works best for dynamic wrinkles — lines that appear when you make facial expressions. If your concerns are static wrinkles (visible at rest), volume loss, or skin texture, other treatments may be more effective.

## The Fundamental Difference

Botox and fillers are the two most popular non-surgical anti-aging treatments — but they work in completely different ways, treat different problems, and are not interchangeable.

Botox relaxes muscles. When the muscle can't contract as forcefully, the wrinkle it creates softens. This is why Botox is best for dynamic wrinkles — forehead lines, frown lines, and crow's feet that appear when you make facial expressions.

Fillers add volume. Dermal fillers like Juvederm are gel-like substances (usually hyaluronic acid) injected beneath the skin to fill in wrinkles, plump lips, restore cheek volume, or contour the jawline. They work on static wrinkles — lines and hollows visible even when your face is at rest.

## When to Choose Botox

Botox is your treatment if you're bothered by lines that appear when you move your face. The lines across your forehead when you raise your eyebrows. The vertical "11" lines between your brows when you concentrate. The crow's feet that fan out when you smile.

Botox also works preventively. Many clients in their late 20s and early 30s start Botox before these lines become permanently etched.

## When to Choose Fillers

Fillers are your treatment if you've lost volume or want to enhance specific features. Lips that have thinned with age. Cheeks that have flattened. Nasolabial folds that have deepened. A jawline that's softened or a chin that needs more definition.

## When You Need Both

The best results often come from combining Botox and fillers. This combination — sometimes called a "liquid facelift" — addresses both muscle-driven wrinkles and volume loss simultaneously.

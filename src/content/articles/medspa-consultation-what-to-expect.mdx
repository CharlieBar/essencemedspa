Your first medspa consultation is one of the most important appointments you'll make for your skin — and one of the most misunderstood. Many people arrive nervous, unsure of what to say or what will happen. Others arrive having already decided exactly what they want, only to discover (with some relief) that there's a better option for their specific concerns. A few arrive expecting a sales pitch and leave pleasantly surprised.

Whatever brings you through our doors at Essence Medspa & Wellness Center in Chicago's Edgebrook neighborhood, the goal of your first consultation is the same: to understand your skin, your goals, and your concerns well enough to create a treatment plan that actually works for you. Here's exactly what that looks like from the moment you arrive.

## Before You Arrive: What to Do to Prepare

You don't need to do much to prepare for your first medspa consultation, but a few things will help us make the most of your time.

**Come with clean skin if possible.** Heavy foundation and concealer can obscure the skin concerns we're trying to assess. If you can't come bare-faced, that's absolutely fine — we'll work with whatever you're comfortable with.

**Bring a list of your current skincare products.** Knowing what you're already using — especially any prescription products like tretinoin or antibiotics — helps us understand your baseline and avoid recommending anything that could conflict with your current routine.

**Write down your main concerns.** It's easy to forget what you wanted to ask once you're in the room. A short list ensures we address everything that matters to you: the fine lines around your eyes, the hyperpigmentation from last summer, the texture issues that won't resolve no matter what you try.

**Think about your goals — not just what bothers you, but what outcome you're hoping for.** "I want to look refreshed without looking like I've had anything done" is a completely valid goal, and one we hear often. Being specific about the outcome helps us identify the right treatments more accurately.

## Step 1: Welcome and Intake

When you arrive at Essence Medspa, you'll be welcomed and offered a seat in our relaxed reception area. If you haven't already completed your intake form online, you'll fill one out at this point. The intake form covers:

- Medical history relevant to skin and aesthetic treatments (conditions, medications, allergies)
- Previous cosmetic procedures or treatments
- Your current skincare routine
- Areas of concern and treatment goals
- Any sensitivities or reactions you've experienced before

This information isn't just administrative — it's clinically important. Certain medications (like blood thinners) affect how we approach injectable treatments. Certain conditions (like active cold sores or autoimmune disorders) influence what treatments are appropriate and when. Your intake form lets us give you safe, personalized care rather than a generic menu of services.

## Step 2: The Skin Assessment

Once you're settled in a private room, your esthetician or provider will perform a thorough skin assessment. This is one of the most valuable parts of your visit, even if you've already decided on a specific treatment.

We examine:

- **Skin type**: Dry, oily, combination, or normal — and how consistently it behaves
- **Hydration levels**: Dehydrated skin often masquerades as oily skin and responds very differently to treatment
- **Barrier integrity**: Signs of a compromised barrier include sensitivity, redness, stinging with products, and patchy dryness
- **Active concerns**: Acne, hyperpigmentation, visible pores, fine lines, volume loss, textural irregularities
- **Fitzpatrick skin type**: This classification (I through VI) guides treatment choices, particularly for laser and light-based treatments, where skin tone is a critical safety variable

In Chicago, we see a wide range of skin concerns shaped by the climate — from chronic winter dehydration to sun damage accumulated during the warmer months. Understanding which concerns are seasonal and which are structural helps us prioritize your treatment plan.

## Step 3: The Conversation About Goals

After the assessment, we have a direct conversation about what you're hoping to achieve. This is not a pressure situation. We're not going to list every service on our menu and ask which ones you'd like. We're going to ask you questions and listen.

Common questions we ask:

- "What's the one thing you wish you could change about your skin?"
- "Are you looking for maintenance, or are you dealing with a specific concern that's getting worse?"
- "Do you have an event or occasion coming up, or is this more about long-term improvement?"
- "What's your comfort level with downtime?"
- "Have you tried anything before that worked, or that didn't?"

Your answers shape everything that follows. Someone who wants immediate results with minimal downtime is a great candidate for treatments like our [custom facial](/services/custom-facial) or the [HydraFacial](/services/hydrofacial). Someone comfortable with a few days of social downtime who wants more dramatic, longer-lasting change might be better served by microneedling or injectable treatments.

## Step 4: Treatment Recommendations

Based on your assessment and your goals, your provider will walk you through specific treatment recommendations. Each recommendation comes with an explanation of:

- **What the treatment does** — the mechanism, not just the marketing language
- **What results you can realistically expect** — and when
- **How many sessions are typically recommended** — some treatments produce results in one session, others work best as a series
- **What the experience is like** — including any discomfort, downtime, or aftercare requirements
- **What it costs** — transparently, before you commit to anything

For clients interested in injectables, this is the point where we have a detailed conversation about anatomy and expectations. [Botox](/services/botox) and [Juvederm fillers](/services/juvederm-fillers) are two of our most requested services, and both require a provider who understands facial anatomy deeply. We'll explain exactly what areas can be treated, what the results look like naturally versus overdone, and what the realistic timeline for results is.

We never recommend more treatment than we believe is genuinely beneficial. A good consultation builds trust — and trust is built by honest, measured recommendations, not upselling.

## Step 5: Questions, Concerns, and Pricing

This is your time. No question is too basic. We hear everything from "Is this going to hurt?" to "What happens if I don't like the results?" and we answer all of it honestly.

Some questions we commonly address:

**"How long will results last?"**
It depends on the treatment. Botox typically lasts 3–4 months. Dermal fillers can last 9–18 months depending on the product and placement. Facial treatments produce results that vary based on your at-home routine and how your skin naturally turns over.

**"Is there a minimum age?"**
No strict minimum, but recommendations vary by age and concern. Many clients in their mid-to-late 20s start with preventive Botox. Others focus on skincare optimization until they see specific changes they want to address.

**"What if I just want to start with one thing and see how it goes?"**
That's a completely valid approach and one we support. You don't have to commit to a full treatment plan on day one.

**"Can I book something today?"**
Yes. If a treatment is appropriate and you're ready, we can often schedule your first appointment at the same visit, especially for facial treatments.

## What Happens After Your Consultation

You'll leave with a clear understanding of:

- What treatments are right for your skin and goals
- A realistic timeline for results
- What your at-home routine should look like to support your treatment
- What your investment will be

There's no obligation, no pressure, and no follow-up calls designed to push you toward booking. We want you to make the right decision for you — and we're here when you're ready.

Many clients walk in unsure and walk out having booked their first appointment. Others take a week to think it over and come back. Both are completely fine.

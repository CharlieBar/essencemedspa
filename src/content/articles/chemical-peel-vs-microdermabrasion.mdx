Both treatments resurface the skin, both deliver real, visible results — but they work through entirely different mechanisms, and choosing the right one depends on your skin type, your concerns, and what kind of downtime you're willing to accept.

## How Each Treatment Actually Works

### Chemical Peels: Controlled Cellular Renewal

A chemical peel uses an acid solution — most commonly glycolic acid, salicylic acid, lactic acid, or trichloroacetic acid (TCA) — to chemically dissolve the bonds holding dead and damaged skin cells together. When those bonds break, the outer layers of skin shed in a controlled way, revealing fresher, smoother skin underneath.

The depth of a peel depends on the acid type, its concentration, and how long it's left on the skin:

- **Superficial peels** target the outermost epidermis. Minimal downtime, mild radiance improvement.
- **Medium-depth peels** reach into the upper dermis. Visible peeling for several days, significant improvement in tone and texture.
- **Deep peels** penetrate further still. Reserved for more advanced concerns and require meaningful recovery time.

At Essence Medspa, our [custom chemical peel](/services/custom-peel) treatments are tailored to your specific skin analysis — we don't apply a one-size-fits-all formula to every client.

### Microdermabrasion: Physical Exfoliation Without Chemicals

Microdermabrasion takes a mechanical approach. A handheld device delivers a controlled stream of fine crystals (or uses a diamond-tipped wand) across the skin surface while simultaneously vacuuming away the loosened dead cells. The result is immediate — smoother, softer skin with a subtle glow — and there's essentially no downtime.

Our [microdermabrasion facial](/services/microdermabrasion-facial) at Essence also incorporates gentle suction that stimulates circulation, giving skin a temporary but noticeable lift in luminosity after a single session.

## When a Chemical Peel Is the Better Choice

Choose a chemical peel when you're targeting concerns that live deeper than the surface:

### Hyperpigmentation and Uneven Tone

Sun spots, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation from acne, and melasma all involve excess melanin deposited below the surface layer. A properly formulated peel — particularly one with glycolic or kojic acid components — can interrupt melanin production pathways and accelerate the turnover of pigmented cells in ways that microdermabrasion simply cannot reach. This is the territory where our [glow and brightening facial protocols](/services/facial-treatments/glow-brightening) often pair with peel series — and where [pigmentation and tone](/concerns/dark-spots) is the underlying concern most clients are trying to correct.

### Active Acne and Clogged Pores

Salicylic acid peels are oil-soluble, which means they can penetrate inside the pore lining and dissolve the sebum and debris that cause breakouts. If you're dealing with congested skin, blackheads, or inflammatory acne, a peel specifically formulated for acne-prone skin is far more effective than mechanical exfoliation. For the full picture of how peels integrate into an acne plan, our [acne and clarity facial cluster](/services/facial-treatments/acne-clarity) maps out the common sequencing, and [our deep dive on the best facial for acne](/blog/best-facial-for-acne) explains what a dedicated acne protocol adds alongside peels.

### Fine Lines and Early Textural Aging

Chemical peels stimulate collagen production in the dermis as the skin heals. This makes them genuinely anti-aging in a way microdermabrasion is not — you're not just removing dead surface cells, you're triggering cellular repair processes that improve skin thickness and elasticity over time.

### When You Can Accept a Few Days of Downtime

If your schedule allows for a week of looking a bit "sun-kissed" before the peel flakes away and reveals fresh skin, the results are worth it. Many Edgebrook clients time their peels around quieter work weeks or before a special event 10–14 days out.

## When Microdermabrasion Is the Better Choice

Microdermabrasion is the right call when you want effective results with zero disruption to your week.

### Maintenance Between Deeper Treatments

Many clients use microdermabrasion as their regular monthly "reset" — clearing congestion, smoothing texture, and keeping skin looking its best between deeper peel sessions or more advanced treatments. It's ideal for maintenance.

### Sensitive Skin That Doesn't Tolerate Acids Well

Some skin types — particularly those with rosacea-prone or highly reactive skin — don't respond well to the chemical agents in peels. Microdermabrasion's physical approach sidesteps the acid sensitivity entirely while still delivering meaningful exfoliation.

### Rough Texture and Surface Dullness

If your main complaint is that your skin just looks flat and feels rough to the touch — no deep pigmentation, no acne scarring, just a loss of that natural glow — microdermabrasion addresses that immediately and effectively. It's also excellent for smoothing out dry, flaky patches that accumulate in Chicago's colder months.

### First-Time Treatment Clients

For clients who've never had professional skin treatments before, microdermabrasion is a gentle, confidence-building entry point. There's nothing to fear, no recovery, and the results after just one session often convert skeptics into regulars.

## Practical Tips Before Your Treatment

**Before a chemical peel:**
- Discontinue retinoids (Retin-A, tretinoin) for 5–7 days prior
- Avoid waxing the treatment area for at least 48 hours beforehand
- Increase SPF use in the weeks before — sun-damaged skin is more reactive
- Arrive with clean, product-free skin on the day of your appointment
- Plan your social calendar: medium-depth peels will cause visible flaking for 4–7 days

**Before microdermabrasion:**
- Avoid active sunburn or significant sun exposure the day of treatment
- Skip any harsh exfoliants (scrubs, acids) 48 hours before
- Come with clean skin — no heavy makeup
- Moisturize well in the days leading up to your appointment if your skin tends to be dry

**After either treatment:**
- SPF is non-negotiable. Freshly exfoliated skin is photosensitive, and UV exposure will undo your results
- Gentle, fragrance-free skincare only for the first few days
- No picking, rubbing, or pulling at peeling skin — let the process happen naturally
- Hydrate internally: water intake genuinely affects how well your skin recovers
